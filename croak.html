Background

This is a four-day hack project. We aim at providing more effective communication in the workplace. The project was one of the hightlights in the presentation, and we won best presentation award.

The Problem

People work remote nowadays. It's cheaper to hire in a variety of locations, and employers have access to a wider talent pool.

Over 13.4 million work remote in America alone, and the number is increasing. Over next 9 years, 41% of workforce will be working remote.
Forbes: 10 Ways Millennials Are Creating The Future Of Work

Friction.

Communication is not efficient. Setting up GoToMeeting and WebEx takes too long.

Non-verbal cues are missing. When a meeting is over, conversation is cut off.

Approach

We had an idea of a always-on-video, which enables frictionless communication for remote and distributed teams, by recreating the "tap on the shoulder" experience that occurs within the physical workplace. We started with brainstorming and looking at existing products. It seems a coincidence that some startup have the similar great ideas, and there's few products out there that streams live video feed or take photos of the user every couple minutes. But why are those great products not widely used?

Low-fi Prototype and Customer Focus

I quickly made a working prototype of our idea. I wanted to bring it to customers and listen to what they think.

We talked to 7 potential users in customer panel. We learned that people loved this idea. However, some concerns over privacy issues are also mentioned:

 

Challenges

  • Intrusive
  • Micromanagement
  • Embarrassment
  • Hard to get everyone on it

What we changed / features

  • Target highly collaborative environment and teams
  • Provide Photo of The Day option, as opposed to consistent video feed or photo taken every couple seconds
  • Provide pause feature for focus time
  • Target millenial workers who have more willingness to allow access to their personal data or web behavior, and a greater interest in cooperating with Internet businesses

 

Data Synthesis

We mainly used flow model, sequence model, and affinity diagrams to synthesize our data.

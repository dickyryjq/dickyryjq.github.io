Mobile Collaboration in the Workplace

To enable people to interact with one another as naturally over technology as they do in person.

What do we lose during a remote collaboration that would come naturally during face-to-face? Can technology replace unspoken cues body language and the environment? In 8 months, we research and create a design solution that encourages ad-hoc micro-collaborations in the enterprise 5-10 years in the future.
This is a MHCI capstone project, and our client is Cisco System, inc.

Research
Due to the confidential nature of this project, I cannot share our ideas and designs. Nevertheless, I will document the process throughout the project.
After a kick-off meeting in with Cisco in January, we began our four-month research, which was divided into three phases.

Phase 1 - Lay of the land
We started by observing MHCI project team meetings, and from the data we extracted questions for us to move forward.

Phase 2 - Into the Real World
We went to local Pittsburgh companies, co-working spaces, and theaters, and had a very productive interview experience. From the horse's mouth, we learned key issues and again we synthesized the data for us to move on to the next phase.
Phase 3 - Deep Dive at Cisco
The team flew to Cisco offices in California. With some unique and creative research methods we used, we were able to generate key findings from a huge volume of data we collected.
